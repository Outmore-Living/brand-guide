# Outmore Living — Brand Identity

---

## Who We Are

Outmore Living makes heated outdoor furniture. We extend life beyond four walls so every season is your season. Founded when co-founder Kevin Long sat outside on a chilly evening and watched everyone head indoors. The question was simple: why can't our seats be heated? Prototypes in a garage, studying heated car seats, testing batteries. The first prototype secured funding, patents, designers, and engineers.

**Tagline:** Warmth, Without Walls.

---

## Mission

Extend life beyond four walls.

Comfort shouldn't end at the door. It should follow you outside — into the crisp morning air, the golden hour sunset, the star-filled night. We create heated outdoor furniture that makes patios, decks, and gardens year-round living rooms.

## Vision

A world where the outdoors is as comfortable as indoors. Where technology disappears into beautiful furniture and warmth just happens.

---

## Core Values

### 1. Comfort First
Everything starts with how it feels. Not specs, not features — the experience of sitting down and not wanting to get up. If it doesn't feel amazing, it doesn't ship.

### 2. Design With Intention
Every detail earns its place. No ornamentation for the sake of it. Minimalist clarity in products and interfaces. Inspired by Apple's restraint, Mercury's speed, Superpower's elegance, Aesop's warmth, and Dieter Rams' discipline.

### 3. Built for Outside
We don't adapt indoor furniture for outdoors. We engineer from the ground up for weather, durability, and the elements. Outdoor is our domain.

### 4. Warmth in Everything
Warmth isn't just our product feature — it's our brand, our voice, our customer experience. From heated cushions to warm customer interactions. Every touchpoint should feel like coming home.

### 5. Premium, Not Exclusive
Elevated doesn't mean inaccessible. Premium quality, clear pricing, no pretension.

## Voice & Tone

Active voice, always. Short sentences that punch. Lead with benefits, not features. Conversational but professional — like a knowledgeable friend. Say "you" more than "we." No jargon, no buzzwords, no corporate speak.

### Yes
> "Sit outside in January. Seriously."

> "Warmth, built in. No cords, no fuss."

### No
> "Our innovative heating technology provides thermal comfort in sub-optimal weather conditions."

> "Featuring integrated heating elements with cordless power delivery systems."

### Tone by Audience

| Audience | Tone |
|----------|------|
| **Consumer** | Aspirational, sensory, lifestyle-focused. Paint the picture of the experience. |
| **Trade / Hospitality** | Professional, partnership-focused, ROI-aware. Speak to extending guest experience and seasonal revenue. |
| **Interior Designers & Architects** | Technical credibility, material focus, specification-ready. |

---

## The "Be Outmore" Test

Before shipping anything — a product, a page, an email, a feature — ask:

1. **Does it extend life beyond four walls?** (Purpose)
2. **Does it feel warm?** (Brand)
3. **Is it clear and intentional?** (Design)
4. **Would I use this outside in November?** (Product truth)
5. **Is it something we're proud of?** (Quality bar)

If any answer is no, iterate.

## Beliefs

- The outdoors is underutilized. Most people use their outdoor space 4--5 months a year.
- Technology should be invisible. The best tech disappears. You shouldn't think about the heating element — you should just feel warm.
- Comfort drives connection. When people are comfortable, they stay longer, talk more, and make memories.
- Quality compounds. A well-made product earns trust, generates referrals, and builds a brand.
- Direct is better. Direct relationships with customers mean better products, faster feedback, and genuine connection.

## Anti-Patterns

Things that are never Outmore:

| Anti-Pattern | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Neon colors or saturated blues | Cold, tech-bro aesthetic. Not us. |
| Sharp corners everywhere | Feels corporate and rigid. |
| Heavy drop shadows | Dated, heavy. We use subtle elevation. |
| Centered walls of text | Creates scanning fatigue. Left-align body copy. |
| Carousel sliders | Users don't click past slide 1. Use a grid. |
| Autoplay video | Intrusive. Let the user choose to engage. |
| Popup modals on page load | Hostile to the user experience. Earn attention first. |
| Small, light gray text | Accessibility failure. Contrast matters. |
| Hamburger menus on desktop | Navigation should be visible, not hidden. |
| Jargon and buzzwords | If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it. |
